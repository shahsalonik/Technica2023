Welcome to WeCycle!

Globally, about 2.01 billion tons of trash is generated per year. To prevent all of this from going into landfills, we sort items as either trash or recycling. Trash is what cannot be reused, and what will end up in a landfill. Recycling is what can be reused, and ends up in special centers that process them and turn them into something new. However, what happens when trash ends up with recycling, or vice versa? When trash ends up with recycling, it has the potention of spoiling all of the recycling with it. When this occurs, what once could've been reused will also end up in a landfill. As for when recycling ends up with trash, it goes directly to a landfill. Once again, what once could've been reused will now end up in a landfill. This highlights the importance of properly sorting both trash and recycling.

Only a third of total recyclable material generated by American households is actually recycled.

However, do you know how to properly sort trash and recycling?

While this may seem intuitive to many, there are actually many small details that people forget about when sorting an item as trash or recycling. For example, did you know that certain types of plastic that cannot be recycled? Or that the sticky parts of sticky notes is trash, while the rest is recycling? Regardless of if you consider yourself someone who knows everything about recycling, or someone who has never given it much thought, it's important to practice identifying whether something is trash or recycling.

To help with this, we introduce: WeCycle!

WeCycle is a program that gamifies classifying items as trash or recycling! Read through our website to find out more how you can help create a more sustainable future, and take our quiz! These 25 questions will test your knowledge of what you've learned. Try to earn the maximum score!
